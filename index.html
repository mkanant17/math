Master's Thesis (Aug 2024 - Present), IISc, Bangalore

Supervisor: Purvi Gupta

My thesis focuses on exploring Hilbert geometry through the lens of computational geometry, and is divided into two parts. The first part is a joint work with P. Gupta. For a bounded convex domain $K\subset \mathbb{R}^d$, we propose an approximate membership query for the associated convex floating bodies, where the error is measured by the affine-invariant Hilbert metric on $K$. We produce an efficient data structure to answer this query, which relies on a comparison between convex floating bodies and Hilbert metric balls. The second part, which I am currently working on, involves studying the Voronoi diagrams in the Hilbert metric on convex polygons, and is based on a work by Gezalyan and Mount (2021).

  • Volume Entropy of Hilbert Metrics via Flag Approximability of Convex Bodies ReportSlides

Master's Project (Jan 2024 - Jun 2024), IISc, Bangalore

Supervisor: Purvi Gupta

For my project, I studied a proof of the entropy upper bound conjecture for Hilbert geometry, by Vernicos and Walsh (2018). It involves establishing a concrete relationship between volume entropy and flag approximability, where the latter is a combinatorial measure of complexity of polytopes that approximate a given convex body in the Hausdorff metric. To prove one part of this relation, I studied a paper by Arya, da Fonseca, and Mount (2016) which provides a way to control the combinatorial complexity of approximating polytopes using the Busemann volume of Hilbert metric balls. The other part involved exploring the Hilbert metric from the perspective of Finsler geometry.
